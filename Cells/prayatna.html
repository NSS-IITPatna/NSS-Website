Vision

To develop humble individuals having a sense of gratitude with the way they had their education and in turn think bigger than themselves by contributing back in whichever small/big way possible. This will create an unending chain of compassionate individuals which will make a healthy society and slowly but steadily a healthy nation.

Mission

Demographically with average age of 29, India will be the youngest country by 2020. This puts us in great advantageous situation if the youth is given right direction otherwise we can be a disaster as well. Financially stable families send their children to private schools complementing their studies with tution and coaching. But a major chunk of our population cannot afford money for two full meals, let alone education. 65% of the school-going students of our country study in government schools majority of which are in pathetically poor conditions. 2 To bridge this gap Prayatna aims to give best possible free education to children of economically weaker sections with well defined syllabus, covered by knowledgeable teachers within stipulated time so that these students too can stand at-par with students of private schools. Along with the bookish education we also provide students with moral values, mental support making them capable of taking their own career decisions while also inculcating self-confidence in them at the same time. This program mutually benefits the students of IIT Patna, as it helps in overall personality development when they act as a responsible teacher.
